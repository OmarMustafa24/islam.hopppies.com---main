Islam is a monotheistic Abrahamic religion founded by the Prophet Muhammad in the 7th century CE in the Arabian Peninsula. It is one of the major world religions, with over a billion followers, known as Muslims, worldwide.

The central religious text of Islam is the Quran, believed by Muslims to be the word of God as revealed to Muhammad over a period of approximately 23 years through the angel Gabriel. Alongside the Quran, Muslims also follow the teachings and practices of Muhammad, known as the Hadith.

The Five Pillars of Islam are the five basic acts that are considered obligatory for all Muslims. They are:

  1. Shahada: The declaration of faith, affirming that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is his messenger.
  2. Salah: The ritual prayer performed five times a day facing the Kaaba in Mecca.
  3. Zakat: Almsgiving or charity, where Muslims are required to give a portion of their wealth to those in need.
  4. Sawm: Fasting during the month of Ramadan, from dawn until sunset.
  5. Hajj: The pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca, which all Muslims who are physically and financially able are required to undertake at least once in their lifetime.

In addition to these pillars, Islam provides guidance on various aspects of life, including personal conduct, family life, social justice, and spirituality. It emphasizes the importance of submission to the will of God (Allah), compassion, justice, and community welfare. Islam also encompasses a rich cultural and intellectual tradition, with contributions in fields such as science, mathematics, philosophy, and art throughout history.
